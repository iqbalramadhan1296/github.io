Research

Explaining Democratic Backsliding in Indonesia (2019–2024): Structural Conditions and Power Consolidation under Joko Widodo

Master’s Thesis, International University of Japan, 2025

  • Analysed the causes of democratic backsliding in Indonesia using a structural approach and a case study of President Joko Widodo.
  • Methodology: Qualitative analysis, literature review, and in-depth interviews.
  • Findings: Identified three key factors: institutional weakening, executive power consolidation, and opposition/ally’s co-optation.

Abstract

This thesis examines how Joko Widodo (Jokowi), once regarded as the face of Indonesian democracy, becomes the principal perpetrator of its democratic backsliding during his second term of presidency. Combining Luo and Przeworski’s framework on democratic backsliding with Levitsky and Ziblatt’s concept of subverting democracy, this research examines Indonesia's structural conditions, particularly the multiparty presidential system, that enable Jokowi’s consolidation of power.

It employs qualitative analysis of political discourse, electoral data, and legal documents to identify key mechanisms through which democratic norms are eroded. The findings show that Jokowi’s actions not only consolidate his influence but also systematically weaken Indonesia’s democratic institutions, setting a troubling precedent for future leaders.

This study contributes to the broader discourse on democratic backsliding by illustrating how elected leaders, under favourable structural conditions, can incrementally undermine democratic norms.
