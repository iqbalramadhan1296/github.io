Research

Explaining Democratic Backsliding in Indonesia (2019–2024): Structural Conditions and Power Consolidation under Joko Widodo

Master’s Thesis, International University of Japan, 2025
  • Analysed the causes of democratic backsliding in Indonesia using a structural approach and a case study of Joko Widodo's presidency.
  • Methodology: Qualitative analysis, literature review, and in-depth interviews.
  • Findings: Identified three key factors: institutional weakening, executive power consolidation, and opposition/ally’s co-optation.

Survey on Political Dynamics in Depok ahead of the 2024 Election

Freelance, 2023
  • Conducted a survey on local political dynamics in Depok for candidates running for the Depok City Regional House of Representatives (DPRD).
  • Methodology: Online survey and focus group discussion.

Market Survey on Toothpaste Usage Patterns in the Five Largest Cities in Indonesia

KedaiKOPI Research Agency, 2021
  • Survey on toothpaste usage patterns in five major cities in Indonesia to support a toothpaste company in launching a new product.
  • Methodology: Face-to-face survey.

Survey on Stunting Prevention Communication in the Seven Most Severely Affected Regions

KedaiKOPI Research Agency, 2021
  • Public communication survey on stunting prevention in the seven regions with the most severe stunting cases in Indonesia.
  • Methodology: Face-to-face survey and focus group discussion.
  • Findings: Lack of awareness regarding when stunting can be prevented and when it is irreversible was the main cause behind high cases.

Survey and Focus Group Discussions on Political Dynamics Ahead of the 2024 Election in Java and Madura

KedaiKOPI Research Agency, 2021
  • Public opinion survey and FGD with experts from each electoral district in Java and Madura about political dynamics leading to the 2024 elections.
  • Methodology: Face-to-face survey and focus group discussion.
  • Findings: Urban areas emphasised government service improvements, while rural areas highlighted better living standards.

National Survey on the Relocation of the Capital to Kalimantan

KedaiKOPI Research Agency, 2019
  • Analysed public opinion nationwide about President Joko Widodo’s plan to relocate the capital from Jakarta to East Kalimantan Province.
  • Methodology: Face-to-face interviews.
  • Findings: 39.8% disapproved, 35.6% approved, 24.6% had no opinion. 95.7% of respondents from Jakarta strongly disapproved.
